## Use bytes32 rather than string/bytes. ~300 gas (with optimization we spend 314 gas less while without it we spend 426 gas)  If you can fit your data in 32 bytes, then you should use bytes32 datatype rather than bytes or strings as it is much cheaper in solidity. Basically, Any fixed size variable in solidity is cheaper than variable size. [From the docs](https://docs.soliditylang.org/en/v0.5.8/types.html#bytes-and-strings-as-arrays), As a general rule, use bytes for arbitrary-length raw byte data and string for arbitrary-length string (UTF-8) data. If you can limit the length to a certain number of bytes, always use one of the value types bytes1 to bytes32 because they are much cheaper.
